Developement of a Real-Time Structural Monitoring System

Anonto Zaman

Introduction

In recent years, policymakers have noted that the infrastructure of the United States desperately needs updating. Despite recent developments in sensor technology, there is not yet an established method of measuring a structure’s integrity in real-time. Strain gauges are a form of instrumentation that are commonly used to measure the amount of force that an object undergoes. By integrating strain gauges into the frame of bridges, the public will be able to quickly determine if it is able to support structural loads.

Background

In its 2017 Infrastructure Report Card, the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) gave the US a D+ grade for its overall infrastructure health. The ASCE highlighted the fact that 9.1% of bridges in the US are structurally deficient, with approximately 188 million trips across a structurally deficient bridge per day. Of the 614,387 bridges in the US, approximately 40% are 50 years or older. Because damage to a structure is difficult to assess, engineers are required manually inspect bridges. This problem is only amplified by recent changes in global climate patterns; a United increasingly common in recent years. The increased presence of natural disasters will continue to take a toll on America’s crumbling bridges and infrastructure. Thus, a system must be developed to prevent bridge failure and protect the public from danger. By first designing a safety system for a bridge, strain gauge technology could eventually be applied to measure the health of other at risk structures, such as cranes, skyscrapers, and dams.

Circuit Design

One of my preliminary circuits for the project was a simple instrumentation amplifier that I built using an AD622. After having some success with a differential amplifier that I built with three LM411 op-amps, I felt that the instrumentation amplifier was the next logical step in successfully transferring data from the strain gauges.

Results and Data

I had great success with my differential amplifier and my instrumentation amplifier. Each circuit was able to successfully amplify the given voltage differential. For my final circuit my goal is to successfully analyze changes in material strain using a mixture of strain gauges and signal analysis circuitry.
